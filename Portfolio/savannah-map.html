Savannah Urban Planning

Sustainable Development and Community Projects

"Savannah's historic squares represent one of America's finest achievements in city planning — a testament to how thoughtful urban design can create enduring, vibrant communities that stand the test of time."

The Oglethorpe Plan

The Oglethorpe Plan, conceived in 1733, represents a masterpiece of urban planning. Its system of squares, each surrounded by civic buildings and residences, created intimate public spaces that continue to define Savannah's character. The plan's genius lies in its scalability and its integration of public spaces with private development.

Each square serves as a small park, a social gathering space, and a focal point for surrounding buildings. This creates a rhythm of open and built spaces that makes Savannah one of the most walkable cities in America.

Architectural Evolution

Savannah's architectural heritage spans three centuries, showcasing styles from Georgian to Greek Revival and Victorian. Each building tells a story of the city's evolution, while maintaining a harmonious relationship with its neighbors and the urban fabric.

The preservation of these architectural treasures, combined with sensitive new development, ensures that Savannah remains a living museum of American urban design.

Contemporary Integration

Modern development in Savannah demonstrates how new construction can respect historical context while meeting contemporary needs. Projects focus on sustainable design principles and maintaining the human scale that makes Savannah unique.

These developments show how historical preservation and modern innovation can work together to create vibrant urban spaces.
